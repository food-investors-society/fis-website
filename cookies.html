Use of Cookies

Last updated: 3 July 2026

1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly and can also be used to understand how visitors use a site.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to support website functionality, remember consent preferences, and understand site usage through analytics.

3. Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are viewed, how long visitors stay, how they arrived at the site, and what type of device or browser they use. This helps us improve the website and our content.

4. Consent

Where required, we will ask for your consent before placing non-essential analytics cookies on your device. You can change your choice later if a cookie preference control is made available on the site.

5. Third-Party Services

Some cookies may be set by third-party services that we use, including Google Analytics. These providers may process information in accordance with their own privacy policies.

6. Managing Cookies

You can usually control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Blocking some cookies may affect how parts of the website work.

7. More Information

For more about how we use personal information collected through the site, please read our Privacy Policy.

8. Updates

We may update this page from time to time to reflect changes to the technologies we use or legal requirements. The latest version will always appear on this page.
